# Pricing Hypothesis — Pilot Reviews

**Status**: HYPOTHESIS — not validated by market feedback

## Pilot pricing

| Tier | Price (INR) | Scope |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Basic review | 10,000 | All 5 rules, single product revision, one evaluation case |
| Extended review | 20,000 | All 5 rules, up to 3 product revisions, comparison report |
| Full engagement | 30,000 | Extended + evidence gap analysis + 1 re-evaluation after design changes |

## Pricing rationale

- **Lower bound (10,000 INR)**: Approximate cost of 4 hours of senior engineering time at typical Indian startup rates. Kernel evaluation takes minutes; time is spent on intake normalization and report writing.
- **Upper bound (30,000 INR)**: Positioned below the cost of a single prototype iteration (estimated INR 5,000-15,000 in materials + 2-3 days of engineering time).
- **Value proposition**: A systematic review that catches issues before money is spent on prototyping.

## What we do not know

- Whether this price range matches willingness-to-pay for the target customer segment
- Whether the deliverable format (JSON + markdown report) meets customer expectations
- Whether customers want a single-shot review or ongoing monitoring
- Whether the 5-rule set covers the subsystems customers most want analyzed
- How many reviews we can deliver per week with the current kernel

## Validation plan

- Offer the first 2 reviews at the basic tier price
- Collect structured feedback via feedback-form.md
- Adjust pricing based on: time-to-complete, perceived value, competitive alternatives

## Explicit non-claims

These prices are hypotheses, not published rates. They may change after pilot feedback. No commitment to future pricing is implied.
